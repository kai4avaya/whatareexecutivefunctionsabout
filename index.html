bodyLITERATE Research & Lesson Planning Tool

Overview

This tool is designed to empower educators, therapists, and researchers working with executive function skills, mindfulness, and somatics. Developed by bodyLITERATE, it serves as a comprehensive platform to track research, organize lesson plans, and support those teaching people of all ages—including individuals with ADHD, processing issues, and neurodiverse profiles.

Key Features

  • AI-Powered Research Extraction: Utilizes Google Gemini, a state-of-the-art large language model, to extract and summarize academic papers and research relevant to executive functions, mindfulness, and somatic practices.
  • Academic Paper Aggregation: Automatically pulls research papers from the ERIC (Education Resources Information Center) database, ensuring access to the latest peer-reviewed studies in education and psychology.
  • bodyLITERATE Research Archive: Integrates years of curated research and resources compiled by the bodyLITERATE Research Initiative (since 2008), providing a unique, expert-driven knowledge base.
  • Lesson Plan Organization: Allows educators to organize, annotate, and link research findings directly to lesson plans, supporting evidence-based teaching and individualized instruction.
  • Support for Diverse Learners: Designed with a focus on executive function skill-building for all ages, including tools and resources tailored for ADHD, learning differences, and neurodiversity.

How It Works

  1. Query & Extraction: Users can ask questions or search for topics related to executive function, mindfulness, or somatics. The app uses Gemini to interpret queries and extract relevant academic content.
  2. Database Integration: The system searches both the ERIC database and the bodyLITERATE research archive, returning summaries and links to full papers where available.
  3. Lesson Planning Tools: Research findings can be organized into lesson plans, annotated, and shared with colleagues, supporting collaborative and adaptive teaching.

Under the Hood

  • AI Model: Google Gemini (for natural language understanding and summarization)
  • Academic Sources: ERIC database, bodyLITERATE Research Initiative archive (2008–present)
  • Target Users: Teachers, therapists, researchers, and anyone supporting executive function development

Methodologies

We see learning as a joyful, embodied process that is incorporated within an individualized emergent system that we construct with each student.

Our process is built upon two fundamental ideas:

  1. Awareness practices, including embodied learning through the Alexander Technique, so that students can identify and track their interests and needs.
  2. Artist inspired reflection practices entail bridging multiple interests, enabling students to select, curate and track the most pertinent information to help them succeed.

Awareness and reflective practices are the framework of an emergent learning system: woven into the fabric of all we do is the love of learning through awareness of interests/needs and building upon past successes within each iteration to create self-sustained learning.
